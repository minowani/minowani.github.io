Saṃyutta Nikāya
Khandhasaṃyuttaṃ
Khajjanīyasuttaṃ
SN22.79

At Sāvatthi.

'For whatever, almsmen, or recluses or divine man remembering various prior abodes, they remember all these five masses-with-grasping together or they remember certain.

Which five?

'Or 'with such form I was in past durations' thus, almsmen, for the remembrance he remembers just the form, or 'with such feeling I was in past durations' thus, almsmen, for the remembrance he remembers just the feeling, or 'with such perception I was in past durations' thus, almsmen, for the remembrance he remembers just the perception, or 'with such inclinations I was in past durations' thus, almsmen, for the remembrance he remembers just the inclinations, or 'with such cognition I was in past durations' thus, almsmen, for the remembrance he remembers just the cognition.

And what, almsmen, would they say is form?

Now thus it deforms, almsmen, from that it is to be called 'form'. Deforms with what? Deforms with cold, deforms with heat, deforms with hunger, deforms with thirst, deforms with gadfly, mosquito, wind, sun, contact with the creeping. Now it thus deforms, almsmen, from that it is to be called 'form'.

And what, almsmen, would they say is feeling?

Now thus it feels, almsmen, from that it is to be called 'feeling'. And what it feels? Happiness it feels, suffering it feels, neither suffering nor happiness it feels. Now it thus feels, almsmen, from that it is to be called 'feeling'.

And what, almsmen, would they say is perception?

Now thus it perceives, almsmen, from that it is to be called 'perception'. And what it perceives? Also dark it perceives, also yellow it perceives, also red it perceives, also light it perceives. Now thus it perceives, almsmen, from that it is to be called 'perception'.

And what, almsmen, would they say are inclinations?

Now thus they incline the inclined, almsmen, from that it is to be called 'inclinations'. And what are the inclined they incline? form for the form-ness is the inclined they incline, feeling for the feeling-ness is the inclined they incline, perception for the perception-ness is the inclined they incline, inclinations for the inclination-ness is the inclined they incline, cognition for the cognition-ness is the inclined they incline. Now thus they incline the inclined, almsmen, from that it is to be called 'inclinations'.

And what, almsmen, would they say is cognition?

Now thus it cognizes, almsmen, for that it is to be called 'cognition'. And what it cognizes? Also sour it cognizes, also bitter it cognizes, also sharp it cognizes, also sweet it cognizes, also soapy it cognizes, also not-soapy it cognizes, also salty it cognizes, also bland it cognizes. Now thus it cognizes, almsmen, for that it is to be called 'cognition'.

There, almsmen, having learned, a hearer of nobleness considers thus:

'Now, I am presently consumed with form,
for periods in the past I was consumed with form just so.
As therefor also presently I am consumed with the current form.
Now, and like I would welcome yet the future form,
for periods in the future I also would be consumed with form just so.
As therefor also presently I am consumed with the current form.'

Thus considering he has no expectation in future form. He does not welcome future form. He opposed, from the disenchantment, from the fading, from the cessation, to the current form.

> Idem with feeling, perception, inclinations, cognition.

Therefor, what do you deem almsmen, is form or permanent or impermanent?'

'Impermanent, venerable.'

'What is yet impermanent is it therefor or sufferable or happy?'

'Sufferable, venerable.'

'What is yet impermanent, sufferable, a principle of change for the worse, is it therefor okey to see: 'This is me, this I am, this is my self?''

'Course not, venerable.'

> Idem with feeling, perception, inclinations, cognition.

'Therefor this, almsmen, 'whatever form, past, future, current, or internal or external, or gross or subtle, or low or high, what is in the distant or in the near, all the form is essentially with right wisdom similarly to be regarded as 'Not this is me, not this I am, not this is my self'.

> Idem with feeling, perception, inclinations, cognition.

This is called, almsmen, a hearer of nobleness who decreases, not increases; who renounces, not grasps; who turns from, not turns to; who disperses, not congregates.

And what does he decreases, not increases? Form he decreases, not increases. Feeling he decreases, not increases. Perception he decreases, not increases. Inclinations he decreases, not increases. Cognition he decreases, not increases.

And what does he renounces, not grasps? Form he renounces, not grasps. Feeling he renounces, not grasps. Perception he renounces, not grasps. Inclinations he renounces, not grasps. Cognition he renounces, not grasps.

And what does he turns from, not turns to? Form he turns from, not turns to. Feeling he turns from, not turns to. Perception he turns from, not turns to. Inclinations he turns from, not turns to. Cognition he turns from, not turns to.

And what does he disperses, not congregates? Form he disperses, not congregates. Feeling he disperses, not congregates. Perception he disperses, not congregates. Inclinations he disperses, not congregates. Cognition he disperses, not congregates.

Seeing so, almsmen, having learned, a hearer of nobleness gets fed up also on form, gets fed up also on feeling, gets fed up also on perception, gets fed up also on inclinations, gets fed up also on cognition. Fed up, he lacks interest. By the fading of passion he is to be emancipated. Upon emancipation, thus emancipated, the knowledge is. He knows 'Birth is barren, the divine life fulfilled, done what ought to be done, no more further state of being'. This is called, almsmen, the almsmen who neither increases nor decreases.

Stood having decreased, he neither renounces nor grasps. Stood having renounced, he neither turns from nor turns to. Stood having turned from, he neither disperses nor congregates. He stood having dispersed.

And what does he neither increases nor decrease?

Stood having decreased form, he neither increases nor decreases it. Stood having decreased feeling... perception... inclinations... cognition, he neither increases nor decreases it. He stood having decreased.

And what does he neither renounces nor grasps?

Stood having renounced form, he neither renounces nor grasps it. Stood having renounced feeling... perception... inclinations... cognition, he neither renounces nor grasps it. He stood having renounced.

And what does he neither turns from nor turns to?

Stood having turned from form, he neither turns from nor turns too it. Stood having turned from feeling... perception... inclinations... cognition, he neither turns from nor turns to it. He stood having turned from.

And what does he neither disperses nor congregates?

Stood having dispersed form, he neither disperses not congregates it. Stood having dispersed feeling... perception... inclinations... cognition, he neither disperses nor congregates it. He stood having dispersed.

Now with the mind so emancipated, almsmen, deities grouped with Inda, grouped with Brahma, grouped with Pajāpati, like from far, they hail the almsman:

Hail you thoroughbred man, hail you highest man.
We are not familiar to what you are relying,
also what radiates.'
