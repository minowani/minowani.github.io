# Rebirth and Reincarnation

We don’t need to consult many buddhist texts to read that, according to them, death is not the end of suffering. Were it so a knife would then already have been a far more easier tool.

Rebirth and reincarnation don’t have this problem, but they share a sameness in the idea that a certain something is carried over (or remains) from one life to another and precisely that is the problem.

Eternalism (*sassatavādā*) and nihilism (*ucchedavādā*) are two extreme views on existence. Eternal is seen as lasting forever and forever means until the end of time. But what then if time ends? Since eternalism opposes nihilism it must uphold time as never ending (eternal) first. The breaking up, disintegration, perishing, of existing-through-times is seen as nihilism.

Rebirth and reincarnation are based on these same underlying misconceptions and belong to the world of extremes. 
They are not about dependent-co-arising; while they can be found outside buddhism, dependent-co-arising is precisely not [1]. Sometimes they are used to counter nihilism but this would really only indicate a delayed nihilism; existing-through-times until no more. Rebirth and reincarnation just show birth not being understood [2]. Thus when dependent-co-arising is explained as existing through times (across lifetimes, rebirth, causality) it is still *sassatavada*/*ucchedavada*.

The world fares towards extremes. It was the Buddha who taught by the middle and a middle simply can not be grouped with an extreme.

**Notes:**

1. For something you have not experienced, heard of, etc. you have no name. If you do have a name it is for a certain experience, but when it is entirely misunderstood the name includes the misunderstanding. Rebirth and reincarnation views do not just indicate life after dead, they must also include a certain (misunderstood) *how*. And their usage reinforce this misunderstanding. With rebirth and reincarnation beings are seen as reborn or reincarnated, yet beings are born.
2. The existing labels of rebirth and reincarnation engrave and masquerade their underlying wrong views and masks dependent-co-arising. Unlike rebirth and reincarnation, which are english interpretations not translations, birth is not just a view; it does not rely on mere belief. Birth already includes the possibility for a next birth (birth is birth) for it too is dependently-co-arisen. If rebirth would be redefined as ‘*birth* again’ it would still be careless since it still requires an explanation, it is *birth* which is not understood, yet now with the danger of being even more misunderstood. You don’t re-eat a next meal either, you eat again. Depending on the context, birth, next birth, future birth, birth again, etc. will all do just fine; there is no need to seek shelter in wrong views.
